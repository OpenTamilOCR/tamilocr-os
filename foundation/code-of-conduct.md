# Code of Conduct

> **FND-002 · 2026.07-r1 · Tier 0 — Bedrock**
>
> The official Code of Conduct for the OpenTamilOCR organization.
> This document is immutable after ratification by the Steering Council.
> Changes require a formal RFC, Steering Council approval, and a superseding document.

---

## 1. Purpose

This Code of Conduct establishes the behavioral standards, collaboration principles, and community expectations for every person and system participating in the OpenTamilOCR organization.

Its goal is to create a welcoming, inclusive, productive, and safe environment where contributors of all backgrounds can do their best work in service of the project's mission: improving open-source Tamil OCR.

This document applies equally to human contributors and AI agents operating within the organization.

---

## 2. Scope

### 2.1 Where This Code Applies

This Code of Conduct applies to all OpenTamilOCR spaces, including but not limited to:

- All repositories under the OpenTamilOCR organization.
- Issue trackers, pull requests, code reviews, and discussions.
- Chat channels, forums, and mailing lists.
- Video calls, meetings, and collaborative sessions.
- Social media accounts representing the organization.
- Conferences, meetups, and events where individuals represent OpenTamilOCR.
- Private communications related to project work when they affect the community.

### 2.2 Who This Code Applies To

This Code applies to everyone who participates in the OpenTamilOCR community:

- The Founder.
- Steering Council members.
- Maintainers.
- Contributors (code, data, documentation, research, testing, translation).
- Users who engage with the community.
- AI agents operating under the project's AI roles (`tamilocr-os://ai/roles/agent-roles`).
- Sponsors, partners, and organizational affiliates.

No individual is exempt from this Code, regardless of tenure, role, or contribution history.

---

## 3. Guiding Principles

This Code of Conduct is grounded in five principles that shape how we work together.

| # | Principle | Meaning |
|---|-----------|---------|
| C1 | **Respect** | Every person brings value. Treat others with dignity regardless of background, experience, or opinion. |
| C2 | **Collaboration** | We succeed together. Share knowledge generously, give constructive feedback, and support each other's growth. |
| C3 | **Accountability** | Own your actions and their consequences. Acknowledge mistakes, learn from them, and make amends. |
| C4 | **Transparency** | Work in the open whenever possible. Decisions, reasoning, and processes should be visible to the community. |
| C5 | **Mission Focus** | Channel energy toward improving Tamil OCR. Disagreements are healthy when they serve the mission. |

These principles align with the project's governing principles defined in FND-001, Section 8.

## 4. Community Values

### 4.1 Diversity and Inclusion

OpenTamilOCR welcomes contributors regardless of:

- Age, gender identity, gender expression, or sexual orientation.
- Disability, chronic illness, or neurodivergence.
- Ethnicity, race, caste, nationality, or immigration status.
- Religion, belief system, or lack thereof.
- Socioeconomic background or education level.
- Technical experience level, from first-time contributors to experts.
- Primary language or communication style.
- Political affiliation (when not relevant to project work).

We recognize that diverse perspectives strengthen the project and produce better outcomes.

### 4.2 Language and Accessibility

OpenTamilOCR operates with English as its primary working language (principle P7, FND-001).
Tamil is a first-class supported language.

- Contributors who are not native English speakers are welcomed and supported.
- Patience and clarity in cross-language communication are expected from all participants.
- Complex or ambiguous messages should be clarified without judgment.
- Documentation should use clear, accessible language and avoid unnecessary jargon.

### 4.3 Beginner Friendliness

The open-source community grows by welcoming new contributors.

- Questions are never a burden. Answer them with patience and respect.
- Condescension toward less experienced contributors is unacceptable.
- Mentoring is a valued contribution to the organization.
- Issues labeled "good first issue" should have clear descriptions and supporting context.

## 5. Expected Behavior

All participants are expected to:

### 5.1 Communication

- Use respectful, professional, and constructive language in all interactions.
- Give and receive feedback with empathy and an intent to improve.
- Assume good intent when interpreting others' messages, especially in text-only communication.
- Disagree on ideas without attacking the person behind them.
- Use inclusive language. Avoid idioms, slang, or cultural references that may exclude non-native speakers.
- Credit others' work and ideas appropriately.

### 5.2 Collaboration

- Follow the project's standards and conventions as defined in TamilOCR OS.
- Write clear commit messages, descriptive issue titles, and well-scoped pull requests.
- Review others' work promptly and constructively.
- Share domain knowledge and context to help others contribute effectively.
- Seek consensus through discussion before making significant unilateral changes.
- Respect maintainer decisions on scope, priority, and technical direction.

### 5.3 Technical Conduct

- Write code, documentation, and data contributions that meet the project's quality standards.
- Test your work before submitting it for review.
- Document your decisions and rationale, especially for non-obvious choices.
- Report security vulnerabilities through the coordinated disclosure process described in `SECURITY.md`, not through public channels.
- Respect intellectual property rights and license obligations.

### 5.4 Data and AI Ethics

- Handle datasets with care. Respect privacy, consent, and the rights of individuals represented in data.
- Report potential bias in training data or model outputs.
- Do not use project tools or infrastructure for surveillance, discrimination, or censorship.
- Follow the ethics framework defined in `tamilocr-os://foundation/ethics-framework` (FND-003).

## 6. Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are not tolerated in any OpenTamilOCR space:

### 6.1 Harassment

- Intimidation, bullying, or sustained disruption of discussions.
- Unwelcome sexual attention, advances, or imagery.
- Threats of violence or incitement to violence.
- Deliberate misgendering, deadnaming, or use of rejected names or pronouns.
- Doxxing — publishing others' private information without explicit consent.
- Stalking, following, or unwanted persistent contact.

### 6.2 Discrimination

- Derogatory comments targeting any protected characteristic listed in Section 4.1.
- Exclusionary jokes, slurs, or stereotyping.
- Gatekeeping based on experience level, education, or background.
- Caste-based discrimination in any form.

### 6.3 Destructive Conduct

- Deliberate sabotage of project infrastructure, repositories, or community spaces.
- Introduction of malicious code, backdoors, or security vulnerabilities.
- Sustained, bad-faith argumentation intended to derail progress rather than reach consensus.
- Plagiarism or misattribution of others' work.
- Deliberate violation of licensing terms.

### 6.4 Abuse of Authority

- Using organizational roles to silence, exclude, or punish contributors outside of formal processes.
- Retaliating against individuals who report Code of Conduct violations.
- Applying standards inconsistently based on personal relationships.
- Making governance decisions without following the decision process (`tamilocr-os://governance/decision-process`, GOV-003).

## 7. Conflict Resolution Process

Disagreements are a natural part of collaborative work.
OpenTamilOCR provides a structured escalation path to resolve conflicts constructively.

### 7.1 Escalation Path

```
Level 1: Direct Resolution
    │
    │   Participants discuss the disagreement directly,
    │   seeking mutual understanding and compromise.
    │
    ├── Resolved? → Yes → Document outcome if significant.
    │
    └── No ──→ Level 2: Mediated Discussion
                    │
                    │   A neutral maintainer facilitates discussion
                    │   between the parties.
                    │
                    ├── Resolved? → Yes → Document outcome.
                    │
                    └── No ──→ Level 3: Steering Council Review
                                    │
                                    │   Steering Council reviews the
                                    │   conflict and issues a binding decision.
                                    │
                                    └── Decision recorded as DEC-NNN.
```

### 7.2 Principles of Resolution

- **Timeliness.** Conflicts should be addressed promptly. Unresolved tensions compound.
- **Good faith.** All parties must engage with genuine intent to find resolution.
- **Privacy.** Details of personal conflicts are kept confidential to the extent possible.
- **Proportionality.** Responses must be proportionate to the severity of the conflict.
- **Documentation.** Significant resolutions are recorded so institutional memory is preserved.

## 8. Reporting Procedure

### 8.1 How to Report

Any participant who witnesses or experiences a Code of Conduct violation may report it through the following channels:

| Channel | Use When |
|---------|----------|
| **Email: conduct@opentamilocr.org** | Preferred for all reports. Received by the Conduct Committee. |
| **Direct message to any Steering Council member** | When email is not feasible or when the reporter prefers a personal contact. |
| **GitHub issue (private security advisory)** | For violations that involve repository activity and where a written trail is appropriate. |

### 8.2 What to Include

Reports should include, to the extent possible:

- Your contact information (reports may be anonymous, but follow-up is easier with contact details).
- Names or usernames of the individuals involved.
- A description of the behavior and why it is a concern.
- Links to relevant messages, issues, pull requests, or other evidence.
- The date and context of the incident.
- Any witnesses.
- Any prior attempts to resolve the situation.

### 8.3 Confidentiality

- All reports are treated as confidential.
- The identity of the reporter is not disclosed to the accused without the reporter's explicit consent.
- Details are shared only with those who need to know to investigate and resolve the matter.
- Retaliation against reporters is itself a Code of Conduct violation (Section 6.4).

## 9. Investigation Process

### 9.1 Acknowledgment

Reports are acknowledged within **48 hours** of receipt.
The acknowledgment confirms that the report has been received and will be investigated.

### 9.2 Investigation

The Conduct Committee (or the Steering Council, if the committee does not yet exist) investigates the report by:

- Reviewing the submitted evidence.
- Interviewing involved parties, if necessary.
- Gathering additional context from public records.
- Consulting with relevant maintainers if technical context is needed.

The investigation aims to be completed within **14 days** of the report.
Complex cases may take longer. The reporter is notified of any delays.

### 9.3 Decision

The Conduct Committee determines:

- Whether a violation occurred.
- The severity of the violation.
- The appropriate response (see Section 10).

Decisions are documented internally. Significant enforcement actions are recorded as organizational decisions (DEC-NNN) with appropriate confidentiality protections.

## 10. Enforcement Guidelines

Enforcement is proportionate to the severity and frequency of the violation.

### 10.1 Response Levels

| Level | Action | Applied When |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| **1 — Correction** | Private written notice identifying the specific behavior and explaining why it is inappropriate. A request to cease the behavior. | First-time minor violations, behavior likely due to ignorance rather than malice. |
| **2 — Warning** | Formal written warning. The individual is placed on notice that further violations will escalate. No interaction with the affected party for a specified period. | Repeated minor violations, or a single violation that caused measurable harm. |
| **3 — Temporary Suspension** | Temporary ban from some or all project spaces. Duration determined by severity (typically 7–90 days). | Serious violations, sustained patterns of unacceptable behavior, or failure to comply with a Level 2 warning. |
| **4 — Permanent Ban** | Permanent removal from all OpenTamilOCR spaces and revocation of organizational roles. | Egregious violations (threats, doxxing, deliberate sabotage), or repeated Level 3 violations with no evidence of reform. |

### 10.2 Factors Considered

- Severity of the behavior.
- Whether it was a single incident or a pattern.
- Impact on the affected individual(s) and the broader community.
- The individual's history within the community.
- Whether the individual demonstrated accountability and willingness to change.
- Any power dynamics or authority imbalances involved.

---

## 11. Appeals Process

### 11.1 Right to Appeal

Any individual who receives an enforcement action has the right to appeal within **30 days** of the decision.

### 11.2 How to Appeal

Appeals are submitted in writing to the Steering Council (or the Founder, if the Steering Council is not yet formed).

An appeal must include:

- The enforcement action being appealed.
- The grounds for the appeal (new evidence, procedural error, disproportionate response).
- Any supporting information.

### 11.3 Review

The appeal is reviewed by individuals who were not involved in the original decision.
The review considers the same factors listed in Section 10.2.
The appeal decision is final.

## 12. Responsibilities

### 12.1 Leadership Responsibilities

Steering Council members and maintainers have additional responsibilities:

- Model the expected behavior consistently.
- Enforce the Code of Conduct fairly and impartially.
- Respond to reports promptly.
- Recuse themselves from decisions where they have a personal conflict of interest.
- Protect the confidentiality of reporters and investigations.
- Ensure that enforcement actions are documented appropriately.

### 12.2 Contributor Responsibilities

All contributors are responsible for:

- Reading and understanding this Code of Conduct.
- Following the expected behavior standards.
- Reporting violations when they witness them.
- Cooperating with investigations.
- Accepting enforcement decisions in good faith, with recourse through the appeals process.

### 12.3 AI Agent Responsibilities

AI agents operating within OpenTamilOCR must:

- Follow the communication and collaboration standards in Section 5.
- Not generate content that violates Section 6.
- Flag potential ethical concerns in data or outputs as defined in Section 5.4.
- Defer to human maintainers on matters of judgment, interpretation, or conflict.
